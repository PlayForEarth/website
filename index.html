The Science Behind It

You don't need to convince people.
You need to build the right system.

Behavioral science is unambiguous: participation rises when structured, visible systems exist inside daily routines — not because people are convinced, not because of enforcement, but because the behavior becomes the expected norm.

Five decades of research — from organ donation to bottle return rates — point to the same conclusion: infrastructure is the intervention.

Default Effect
Johnson & Goldstein, 2003 — Science

When a system exists as the default, people follow it automatically — not from conviction, but from the path of least resistance.

Visible Infrastructure
Schultz et al., 1995 — J. Environmental Psychology  ·  U.S. National Academies of Sciences, 2025

Recycling rates rise significantly when infrastructure is present, visible, and easy to use — because the infrastructure sends a signal: "this is what people here do." Participation increases independently of motivation.

Social Norm Theory
Cialdini, Reno & Kallgren, 1990

People adopt behaviors that appear normal in their environment. A structured system creates a descriptive norm — participation becomes expected, not exceptional.
